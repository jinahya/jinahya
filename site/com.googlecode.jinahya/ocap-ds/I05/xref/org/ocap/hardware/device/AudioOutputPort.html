164      * Set the compression level of the audio device.
165      * Compression reduces the dynamic range of an audio signal by reducing the
166      * gain when the signal level is higher than a given threshold.  
167      * <p>
168      * The compression levels correspond to implementation-specific ratios of
169      * input level to output gain.  The following table describes the compression
170      * levels.
171      * <table border>
172      * <tr><th>Level</th> <th>Description</th></tr>
173      * <tr><td><code>COMPRESSION_NONE</code></td>
174      *     <td>Always a 1:1 ratio of input level to output gain.
175      *         No compression is applied to the output signal</td>
176      * </tr>
177      * <tr><td><code>COMPRESSION_LIGHT</code></td>
178      *     <td>The lightest level of compression.
179      *         Example ratio would be 2:1, where a 2dB change in input level
180      *         is required to effect a 1dB change in output, above the
181      *         threshold.</td>
182      * </tr>
183      * <tr><td><code>COMPRESSION_MEDIUM</code></td>
184      *     <td>An equal or higher level of compression than <code>COMPRESSION_LIGHT</code></td>
185      * </tr>
186      * <tr><td><code>COMPRESSION_HEAVY</code></td>
187      *     <td>The highest level of compression.
188      *         This may be a high enough level so as to be considered <i>limiting</i>.</td>
189      * </tr>
