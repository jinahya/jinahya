5   /**
6    * <code>S3DAlternativeContentErrorEvent</code> is generated to indicate that
7    * "alternative" content may be presenting due to a detected incompatibility
8    * between the selected 3D content and the display device.
9    * The configuration of the Host device determines the nature of the
10   * alternative content presented; e.g., no video or the 3D video as
11   * requested.  If the Host device attempts to present the requested 3D video,
12   * it is possible that it is being incorrectly displayed on the display device.
13   * </p>
14   * A device detects 3D content metadata (i.e., frame packing) based on signaling
15   * as defined in [OCCEP].  The implementation SHALL compare the signaled content
16   * format to HDMI display device capabilities reported in E-EDID and generate this
17   * event as warranted.  This event will be generated due to the following
18   * situations:
19   * 
20   * <ul>
21   * <li> 3D video content selected but 3D video not supported by the HDMI display device.
22   * <li> 3D video content selected but 3D format not supported by the HDMI display device.
23   * <li> 3D video content selected but no display device connected to an HDMI port.
24   * </ul>
25   * 
26   * Such events are not considered selection or presentation failures.
27   * </p>
28   * Note (informative):
29   * <ul>
30   * <li> If 3D video is selected for presentation but the Host device detects
31   * that no display devices are connected to an HDMI port,
32   * it will black out the video and mute the audio on any other connected outputs.
33   * See [HOST 2.1]
34   * <li> If the Host device detects a possible incompatibility between the 3D content
35   * format and the display device, it may black out the video or send the requested
36   * 3D video to the display device, depending on the Host device configuration
37   * per [HOST 2.1] and [MIB-HOST].
38   * </ul>
39   * 
40   */
